All about the character of the Sphynx cat

For millions of people, a cat is a favorite pet. But among these millions there are people who suffer from fur allergies. In this case, the best solution would be to get a sphynx - this is a hairless cat that will not only protect your health, but also create a minimum of trouble when cleaning the apartment. In this case, allergies will not become an obstacle to communication with your beloved pet. But at the same time, you should remember that caring for sphinxes requires a serious and responsible approach: hairless cats are very sensitive to temperature changes. Low temperature is especially dangerous for them. Therefore, if you decide to take care of a sphynx kitten or an adult cat, learn everything about the character and characteristics of these cats.

The nature of the sphynx cat is peaceful: it does not conflict with other animals, easily gets along with all pets in the house, even with dogs. Also, these cats are quite calm about the presence of outsiders in the house.

Caring for sphinxes - what you need to know?

Caring for sphinxes is a rather painstaking process, but your pet will reward you for your care with its devotion and tenderness. The owner needs to take care of the cat's skin, eyes, ears, teeth and claws. Let's dwell on each of these points in more detail.

Skin care

The idea that hairless cats do not need care is considered wrong, in this case, the opposite is true: sphynxes need constant care and thorough care. If the skin is healthy, it is soft and elastic - the same as the character of Sphynx cats. How to achieve this skin condition? Follow these simple procedures:

  • wipe the entire skin of the sphynx with a damp towel every day
  • baby shampoo is the best bath product in the absence of a special bath product
  • do not rub cream or oil into the sphynx's skin after bathing, this harms the condition of the skin
  • after bathing, wrap the cat in a towel and gently wet it, not wipe the skin
